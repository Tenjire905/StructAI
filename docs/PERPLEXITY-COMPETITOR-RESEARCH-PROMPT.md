## Research brief

I am building **StructAI**: a dark-themed, mobile-first Expo/React Native learning app that teaches people to write better prompts and evaluate AI answers. Core loop = short interactive lessons on linear paths, orbs/streaks/daily goals, Focus vs Playful modes, a Prompt Lab for freeform scoring, glossary term peeks, certificates.

I care about **the feeling of learning** that apps like Mimo deliver (progress, habit, “I got smarter this session”) — but my category is **prompting / AI fluency**, not software development.

### Please research and answer:

### A. Category map (2025–2026)
1. List the strongest **mobile or mobile-first** products that teach prompt engineering / AI tool fluency with active practice (not only video courses).
2. Include at least: **Iro AI**, **PromptPal**, **AiQ**, **PromptSpar / PromptFluent / PromptJam / GeraQuest** if still active, plus any stronger emerging apps.
3. For each: platform (iOS/Android/web), free vs paid, lesson style (MCQ vs write-a-prompt vs AI-graded), social/duels, estimated content depth.
4. Separate **libraries** (God of Prompt) and **guides** (Learn Prompting) from **habit apps**.

### B. What creates “I am learning” feeling
From Mimo / Duolingo / top AI-fluency apps, extract the **top 8 mechanics** that make users *feel* learning progress in ≤10 minutes. Rank by impact. Note which are cheap vs expensive to build.

Specifically evaluate:
- XP / ranks / hearts / lives
- Daily challenge as the “one clear job today”
- Instant AI grading of freeform prompts
- Wrong-answer coaching (why + next pattern)
- End-of-session skill summary (“today you practiced X”)
- Streaks + widgets + notifications
- Duels / leaderboards
- Certificates / shareable artifacts

### C. Positioning white space
Given competitors claim “Duolingo for AI”:
1. Where is the market crowded?
2. Where is there white space for a product that emphasizes **structure, evaluation, grounding, hallucination resistance**, dual Focus/Playful pedagogy, and BYOK Prompt Lab — vs tool-touring (ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini how-tos)?
3. What would make a cold App Store user understand StructAI in one sentence?

### D. Threat assessment for StructAI
Assume StructAI already has: ~6 paths, ~188 lessons, dual modes, orbs/streaks/daily goals, Prompt Lab + BYOK, glossary highlights, certificates, guest mode.
1. What do Iro/PromptPal already do better for *learning feeling*?
2. What can StructAI still own in 90 days without matching their content volume?
3. What is the minimum lovable retention loop for prompting apps in 2026?

Return:
1. Competitor table (columns: product, hook, loop, AI grading?, social?, monetization, StructAI threat 1–5)
